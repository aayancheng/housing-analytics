Feature 01
Tokyo vs The World — 30-Year Price Chart
One chart showing Tokyo condo prices alongside Toronto, Hong Kong, and Vancouver since 1995. The flat line vs the hockey sticks — the most important visual in global housing policy. Tokyo prices in 2025 are near their 2000 levels in real terms.
Feature 02
Upzoning: The Policy That Worked
Japan's national zoning law allows residential construction in nearly all areas, preventing any neighbourhood from blocking supply. Annotated timeline of zoning policy vs price levels — the mechanism behind the stability. The lesson every Western city refuses to learn.
Feature 03
Price-to-Income vs Global Cities
Tokyo's price-to-income ratio vs Hong Kong, Vancouver, Toronto, and Sydney. At ~7.5×, Tokyo is the only global megacity with a ratio approaching what housing economists consider sustainable. How — and whether — that can last as the yen weakens and foreign buyers arrive.
Feature 04
Neighbourhood Breakdown
Minato, Shibuya, Shinjuku, and Setagaya side by side — premium neighbourhoods vs Tokyo averages. The emerging two-tier market: central wards where foreign and wealthy Japanese buyers are pushing prices up, vs the broader city where depreciation is a feature, not a bug.
